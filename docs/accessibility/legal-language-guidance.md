# Legal and Procurement-Sensitive Language Guidance

This document is not legal advice. It is guidance for safer product, sales, and support language based on the current technical evidence.

## Avoid these statements

- “Fully ADA compliant”
- “Fully WCAG compliant”
- “100% accessible”
- “Certified accessible”
- “Fully compliant with all accessibility standards”

## Safer alternatives

- “DocsBot has been evaluated against WCAG 2.1 AA criteria for the chat widget experience covered in our current report.”
- “DocsBot supports many accessibility requirements in the evaluated widget scope, with ongoing improvements.”
- “We can provide our current accessibility conformance report draft upon request.”
- “Accessibility testing and remediation are ongoing, including additional manual and assistive-technology validation.”

## Usage guidance

- Scope statements carefully. If the evidence is widget-only, say widget-only.
- Prefer `supports` or `partially supports` over absolute claims.
- Do not imply third-party certification unless one has actually occurred.
- Do not convert a technical draft VPAT into a blanket legal conclusion.
